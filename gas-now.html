Frequently Asked Questions About Gas Fees

What are gas fees?

Gas fees are costs paid to process transactions or execute smart contracts on blockchains like Ethereum. They act as a fee to use the network.

Why do gas fees increase?

Fees rise when network demand is high. The more congested the blockchain, the more users have to pay to get their transactions confirmed faster.

Whatâ€™s the difference between Gwei and USD?

Gwei is a unit used to measure gas on Ethereum. The USD value represents an estimate of the gas cost in U.S. dollars based on current crypto prices.

How can I save on gas fees?

You can save by transacting during off-peak times, manually setting lower gas limits, or using Layer 2 solutions like Polygon.

Do other blockchains charge gas fees?

Yes. Networks like Binance Smart Chain (BNB) and Polygon (MATIC) also charge fees, usually lower than those on Ethereum.
